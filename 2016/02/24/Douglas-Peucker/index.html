    • 3D Douglas-Peucker

    Furui Sun and Chiyuan Gu
    The object is to Develop a tool which works on 3D polylines or 3D polygons, detects sections straight in xy, and subsequently filters vertices with little influence on the 3D appearance of the feature. The input feature class and the allowed z variance are user-defines inputs to the program. The program outputs weeded 3D features.

    Main algorithm

    We calculated the distance between two points on the plane by x and y coordinates and considered the distance as new x value and z as y value.



    Arcpy functions and Douglas-Peucker algorithm were applied to process the input point data.
